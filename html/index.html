a little Boidie told me
Natural Selection, Virtual Darwinism, and Twitter 
Dan Rahmel & Nikki Purcell
Our project is ...
... a revolutionary way of applying machine learning 
to content posting and channel creation.
Applying the principles of natural selection that nature 
has used for millions of years to the problem of: 
  • Selecting content
  • Making content
  • Publishing content
To help us with this project
meet our cute little guy called a boidie.
What does a boidie do?
Like you and me, all day long he posts to social networks.
  • Tweets
A boidie
  • Retweets
  • Waits to tweet
  • Follows
  • Comments
  • Favorites
  • and Unfollows
Also like you and me 
a boidie has genes that determine
whether he has brown eyes or blue eyes
Whether he is an early riser . . . 
. . . or prefers to make his first
tweet at night
Unlike you and me
A boidie has genes that determine  whether he:
Likes to tweet or retweet
Likes to follow people or 
unfollow people
Likes to be an agressive
poster or a timid poster
Likes to comment or not 
comment on the tweets of others
A boidie does one other main thing...
Boidies like to mate!
They are driven to mate and have little baby boidies.
And this is where our system comes in . . .
And this is where it gets interesting
Each generation of boidies may:
  • Tweet
  • Follow
  • Comment
  • Favorite
You might ask:
That's the sad part of our tale - they don't have 
any babies so they don't pass on their bad genes
That's natural selection!
What happens to the boidies that aren't successful?
We've created a system
Where the most successful boidies combine and
have children with the most successful traits that
then make THEM the most successful
And So On ...
And So On ...
And eventually
We get the Super Boidie of content generation
We've built this system
and it's running right now!
Boidies are tweeting, sleeping, and mating
We are on the 9th generation of
boidies in two days...
... and already we are seeing promising results
What do we have at the end?
We have a generation of boidies that, by natural
selection, have proven themselves the:
  • Best at selecting content
  • Best at posting content at the optimum
  • Gets the most followers
time for maximum effect
  • Gets the most retweets
  • Gets the most comments
...with potentially hundreds of thousands of followers

  • Publishing channel
  • Hundreds of thousands of followers

the types of content that people want
